## 🎯 1. KM Problem Identification (S1-KM-01)

Based on the research analysis, the primary knowledge gap for students is the inability to convert raw personal data into actionable **self-knowledge**. We have identified four critical "KM Pain Points":

* **Information Overload & Cognitive Depletion:** An overwhelming volume of unfiltered data exceeds a student's processing capacity, leading to anxiety and "inefficient learning."
* **The Tracking-to-Understanding Gap:** Users often generate "big data" but lack the systematic understanding to map tool-generated data to daily activities.
* **Siloed & Fragmented Information:** Health information is fragmented across disconnected technologies (e.g., sleep in one app, mood in another), making it the user's difficult responsibility to organize a longitudinal record.
* **Failure of Reflection:** Without data aggregation, students cannot correlate disparate indicators, preventing a holistic view of well-being.

---

## ⚖️ 2. KM Framework Evaluation (S1-KM-02)

### 👥 Considered: Communities of Practice (Wenger)
While strong for social learning, mental health is often a sensitive, private matter hindered by **stigma and labeling effects**. A communal learning structure may trigger "self-stigma," making users less likely to document honest internal states.

### 🌀 Selected: SECI Model (Nonaka & Takeuchi)
The **SECI Model** is a superior fit because it focuses on the internal conversion of **tacit knowledge** (unspoken feelings/internal states) into **explicit knowledge** (quantifiable data). It prioritizes individual "self-activation" over social group dynamics.

---

## 🚀 3. Application as a "Ba" (Knowledge Space)

Our application functions as a **"Ba"**—a secure, personalized dashboard where the interaction between the user and the system generates new insights.

1.  **Externalization (Fleeting Feelings → Explicit History):** The app converts internal "states" into "quantitative or qualitative representations" (logs). This prevents information from remaining siloed in the student's mind.
2.  **Combination (Aggregating Siloed Data):** The app acts as a "connector" for disparate data sets (e.g., correlating sleep quality with academic stress). This builds a "complex representation" of health that was previously invisible.
3.  **Internalization (Self-Awareness):** By engaging in systematic reflection on aggregated data, the student moves from mere tracking to true **self-quantification** and improved health outcomes.
